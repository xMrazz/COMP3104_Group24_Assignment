History: George Brown College was founded in 1967 and is named after George Brown, a prominent Canadian statesman, journalist, and one of the Fathers of Confederation.
Campuses: The college has multiple campuses throughout downtown Toronto, including St. James Campus, Casa Loma Campus(we are here), and Waterfront Campus. Each campus is dedicated to specific programs and fields of study.
Programs: George Brown College offers a diverse array of programs, including diploma and degree programs, postgraduate certificates, and continuing education courses. The programs cover a wide range of fields, including health sciences, business, hospitality, culinary arts, technology, design, and more.
Student Life: George Brown College offers a vibrant student life with various clubs, organizations, and activities to enhance the overall educational experience.
George Brown College is considered a respected institution in Canada's education landscape and continues to play a vital role in preparing students for successful careers in various industries.
